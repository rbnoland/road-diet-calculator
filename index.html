Methodology notes:
KABCO unit costs: K $13,700,000 · A $1,302,300 · B $256,300 · C $122,400 · O $5,500 (USDOT 2024 $). AIS unit costs scaled proportionally. All crash and delay costs are escalated from the 2024 base year to the project opening year, then continue to escalate through the analysis period (VSL 1.07%/yr, VOT 1.6%/yr). Travel delay = AADT × 365 × length × (1/v₂ − 1/v₁) veh-hrs/yr, split into personal (veh-hrs × occupancy × personal VOT) and commercial (veh-hrs × commercial VOT) streams, summed and discounted over the analysis period. Capital cost is a one-time Year-1 expense (not discounted). NPV = PV(crash reduction benefits at chosen reduction %) − capital cost − PV(travel delay costs). Research (FHWA) typically finds road diets reduce crashes 19–47%; central estimate ~29%. BCA parameters follow USDOT guidance: USDOT Benefit-Cost Analysis Guidance.
